about me

Growing up in a low-income household, I encountered financial barriers and navigated personal challenges, but I remained steadfast in my pursuit of learning.

My undergraduate days were heavily scheduled to balance financial needs with academics. Hours commuting to school, multiple low-wage jobs, eating one meal a day, and studying late at night with the bathroom light on and the door slightly ajar was the extent of my family life. But at the end, I had won multiple research scholarships and awards and graduated with a perfect GPA. Part of my coping mechanism had been working hard and lifting my family up. The other part has been the realization that I am privileged to be in Canada because of the vast array of learning opportunities.



After graduating, I had to support my family financially and thus pursued a professional career in the field that I studied.

Five years later, with the help of Future Launch, I am pursuing my aspirations to challenge conventions and achieve my dream of learning French at Université Sainte-Anne.

The 5-week intensive, immersive French program set in a Francophone community in Nova Scotia can be thought of as summer camp for adults. I will be staying in a dormitory, taking French courses, and participating in many extracirricular activities.

why do I want to learn french?

Learning

Learn in a small-group integrative classroom setting

I personally persevered in academic settings and embraced challenges. In French school, each day will be divided into 1) classroom learning and 2) workshops. Activities, such as sports, concerts, whale-watching, kayaking are offered after school and weekends. Upon completion, transferable university credits will be granted, which will be helpful if/when I return to university to upgrade my education.

Empowerment

Empower me to revisit and refine my linguistic abilities

I was born in Quebec but relocated to Toronto at a young age. My household spoke Tamil and Urdu, and I developed a deep appreciation for the nuances inherent in each language. Going to immersion will be exciting as I hope to pick up the language quickly. Nonetheless, I am thrilled to reconnect with my roots and rekindle my passion for the French language and languages in general.

Communication

Enhance my communication skills (transferable skills)

This program brings together students from all walks of life (both Canada and globally) with one goal of learning French. I will learn to navigate diverse social contexts. Interacting with them outside the classroom at meal times, excursions and team sports will help me create bonds. This is essential for personal and professional growth in an increasingly interconnected world.

Future Impact

  • Networking

    Local, national and global opportunities

    Learning French significantly enhances my networking locally and globally. Currently surrounded by Toronto's multicultural landscape, numerous meetups and conferences cater to French speakers. Immersing myself in French-speaking communities expands my professional network while deepening cultural understanding. Additionally, mastering French unlocks national and global career opportunities, especially in francophone cities like those in Quebec. This broadens my horizons, enriches personal and professional life, and amplifies networking capabilities, advancing my career, and exploring new avenues of personal and professional growth.

  • Educational Initiatives

    Teaching assistantship

    Learning French will support me in my aspirations to engage in educational initiatives. I would love to become a language assistant proficient in both French and English. Programs like Odyssey offer ideal platforms for me to apply my teaching eligibility and facilitate language learning in diverse settings. With a background in designing lesson materials, tutoring, and delivering lessons, teaching feels so rewarding and so, I am excited to contribute to language education and foster cross-cultural understanding. By mastering French, I aim to not only enhance my own proficiency but also to inspire and support language learners on their journey to fluency.

  • Friendship

    Building connections

    The lingering feelings of isolation from the pandemic, combined with my current work-from-home situation, have had a profound impact on both my social and mental health.. I never had the chance to experience the camaraderie of dorm life during my years of commuting to university and work. The prospect of participating in this 5-week program, set in a university residence and surrounded by peers from diverse backgrounds and experiences, is incredibly appealing to me. Since our dorms are divided into age groups, this is a unique opportunity to find meaningful connections with individuals who share the same interests and passions. I am eager to cultivate friendships that have the potential to last a lifetime, enriching my personal and social life in ways I have long yearned for.
